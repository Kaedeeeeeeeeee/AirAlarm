Privacy Policy

Effective Date: March 27, 2026

AirAlarm ("we", "the app") is a sleep cycle alarm clock developed by Zhang Shifeng. We are committed to protecting your privacy. This policy explains what data the app handles and how.

Data We Do NOT Collect

AirAlarm does not collect, transmit, or share any personal data. Specifically:

  • We do not collect personal information such as your name, email, or location.
  • We do not use any analytics, tracking, or advertising frameworks.
  • We do not require account creation or sign-in.
  • No data is sent to external servers.

Data Stored Locally on Your Device

The following data is stored only on your device and never leaves it:

  • Sleep Records — Your sleep and wake times, duration, and sleep cycle count are stored locally using Apple's SwiftData framework.
  • User Preferences — Settings such as your preferred language, volume level, bedtime reminder time, and onboarding status are saved in UserDefaults on your device.

AirPods Usage

AirAlarm checks your device's Bluetooth audio route to detect whether AirPods are connected. This is used solely to determine when you fall asleep (by detecting audio interruption when AirPods are removed). No Bluetooth data is recorded, stored, or transmitted.

Notifications

The app may request permission to send local notifications for alarm alerts and optional bedtime reminders. These notifications are scheduled and delivered entirely on your device. No push notification service or external server is involved.

Background Audio

AirAlarm plays white noise and nature sounds using background audio mode. Audio playback occurs entirely on your device.

Third-Party Services

AirAlarm uses no third-party SDKs, analytics services, crash reporters, or advertising networks.

Children's Privacy

AirAlarm does not collect data from anyone, including children. The app is safe for users of all ages.

Changes to This Policy

If we update this privacy policy, the changes will be reflected on this page with an updated effective date.
